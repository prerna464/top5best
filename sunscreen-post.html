☀️ Tips Before You Buy — Read This First

  • SPF tells you UVB protection. PA tells you UVA protection. Always buy SPF 30 minimum and PA+++ or PA++++ for complete protection in Indian sun. A sunscreen with only SPF and no PA rating leaves your skin unprotected from the rays that cause dark spots and ageing.
  • How much to apply matters more than which SPF you choose. Use a full finger-length (roughly ¼ teaspoon) for face and neck. Most people apply only 20–30% of the required amount, which means actual protection is far lower than the SPF number claims.
  • Reapply every 2 hours outdoors. No sunscreen lasts all day. If you are outside — working, commuting, or exercising — reapply every 2 hours. Indoors near windows, once in the morning is sufficient.
  • Matte vs dewy finish. Oily skin does better with matte-finish sunscreens. Dry skin can handle dewy or hydrating formulas. Check the finish before buying.
  • Apply sunscreen as the very last step of your morning skincare — after moisturizer, before makeup. Wait 1–2 minutes for it to set before applying foundation or powder.
